Glee-fully Showcase Hardening Plan

I’m reading your ask as: identify and eliminate anything that could stop glee-fully.tools from functioning as a polished public showcase, living portfolio, and credibility artifact for your AI/product/design capability.

The blunt version: the site is already technically stronger than most personal portfolio sites. The remaining risk is not “does it work?” It is whether it persuades. A portfolio site can pass validators and still fail as a portfolio if visitors cannot quickly understand the work, trust the craft, follow the story, or see proof that the creator can ship.

The repo is already documented as a static HTML/CSS/vanilla JS GitHub Pages site with a central stylesheet, shared JS, search index, Mermaid pages, sitemap, robots policy, and no build step.   That aligns with GitHub Pages’ model: it serves HTML, CSS, and JavaScript from a repository, optionally with a build process.   The existing May 3 audit reports 60 pages scanned, 0 critical issues, 0 warnings, 0 broken internal links, valid manifest/favicons, visible breadcrumbs, skip links, alt coverage, and 2,048 internal links scanned.  

But that does not prove it is a great showcase. It proves the scaffolding is not collapsing.

2. Portfolio failure model

A site can fail as a showcase in five ways:

Failure mode	What it looks like	Damage
Technical failure	Broken links, mobile overflow, slow load, console errors, bad metadata.	Visitor distrust.
Narrative failure	Visitor cannot tell what Glee-fully is, why it exists, or why it matters.	Confusion.
Proof failure	Lots of names and pages, but not enough visible evidence of craft.	“Cute idea” instead of “serious builder.”
Conversion failure	Visitor has no obvious next step: try a tool, support, contact, read more.	Lost opportunity.
Governance failure	Future edits slowly degrade quality, brand, metadata, or hierarchy.	Portfolio rot.

Your plan must defend against all five.
