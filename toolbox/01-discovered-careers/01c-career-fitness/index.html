🌍 Ecosystem · 🪚 Tool #01 – Discovered Careers

Career Fitness

Your career’s personal trainer for skills, titles, and pay.

Career Fitness evaluates your current skills, translates them into real‑world job titles, and surfaces salaries and growth paths that actually fit your life. Paste in a resume, role history, or even rough bullet points and it will map your strengths against roles, flag gaps, and recommend certifications or next steps. Built for thoughtful professionals who want data‑backed clarity before making their next move.

Launch “Career Fitness” in ChatGPT Back to Glee‑fully Discovered Careers

Opens in ChatGPT in a new tab. Best experienced with your notes, lists, or files handy.

What this Tool‑ette does

Career Fitness turns your experience into a clear map of roles, pay ranges, and growth options so you can make informed career moves without starting from a blank page.

Core purpose

Career Fitness combines skill mapping, role matching, and salary insights into a single guided conversation. It helps you turn a messy work history into a focused, evidence‑backed direction for your next move.

Best for

Great for mid‑career professionals, job‑curious employees, and managers planning their next chapter. Especially helpful if you’ve outgrown your current title, want to justify a raise, or are exploring new paths without starting over.

Inputs & outputs

Bring resumes, LinkedIn profiles, job descriptions, or rough bullet‑point histories as input. It returns matched job titles, salary ranges, skill gap summaries, and concrete development plans you can reuse elsewhere.

Core functions (Leaves)

These are the concrete things Career Fitness can do for you inside a single conversation.

  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Skill & history scan: Parses your resume or work history to identify skills, domains, and experience levels worth building on.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Role & title matching: Suggests realistic job titles and career paths that align with your strengths, constraints, and interests.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Salary benchmarking: Helps you reason through salary ranges for target roles using market data, seniority, and location as context.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Upskilling roadmap: Recommends concrete courses, certifications, and practice projects to close the skill gaps it detects.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Long‑term trajectory planning: Turns everything into a phased plan with near‑term moves, stretch roles, and checkpoints you can revisit over time.

Why use this instead of vanilla ChatGPT?

Here’s what you get from a dedicated, suite‑aware GPT instead of a cold, one‑off chat window.

Pre‑tuned brain

Career Fitness is pre‑wired to read resumes, extract skills, infer seniority, and reason about roles and compensation without you having to spell all that out each time. It keeps tone, guardrails, and structure consistent so every session feels like you’re picking up where you left off.

Suite‑aware & ecosystem‑ready

Living on the Glee‑fully Discovered Careers branch, it knows its parent Tool and sibling Tool‑ettes and when to hand you off to them. It speaks the Trunk‑Branch‑Twig‑Leaf language, so its outputs plug neatly into resume, job‑search, and branding workflows across the suite.

Repeatable, export‑friendly workflows

It favors checklists, tables, and named plans over throwaway paragraphs, making it easy to copy into docs, spreadsheets, or your HR systems. You can come back later, paste a previous plan, and have it pick up the thread rather than starting from scratch.

Getting started

Get value in a single session by starting with one document and one clear question.

  1. Step 1 – Bring one snapshot of your career. Start with a single resume, LinkedIn profile, or short list of roles instead of your entire life story.
  2. Step 2 – Paste or upload your history. Drop that content into the chat (optionally alongside a job description) and say what you’re trying to figure out.
  3. Step 3 – Choose a focus path. Ask Career Fitness to match you to roles, sanity‑check compensation, or design a growth plan—and follow its prompts.
  4. Step 4 – Save and reuse the plan. Copy the tables and checklists into your notes or HR tools, and paste them back in later so it can refine rather than restart.
