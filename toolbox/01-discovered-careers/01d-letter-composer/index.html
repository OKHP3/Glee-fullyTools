Letter Composer

Cover letters that sound like you, not a template.

Letter Composer takes the headache out of cover letters. Paste in your resume and a job post, pick a tone, and it drafts a letter that feels professional, specific, and genuinely you — not like it was copied off the internet. Because it lives on the Discovered Careers branch, it plays nicely with Resume Builder, Resume Customizer, and the rest of your job‑search stack.

Launch “Letter Composer” in ChatGPT Back to Discovered Careers

Opens in ChatGPT in a new tab. Best experienced with your resume, job post, and any notes handy.

What this Tool‑ette does

Letter Composer turns your existing resume and a real job posting into a targeted, human cover letter that fits the role.

Core purpose

Letter Composer generates customized cover letters that don’t sound robotic. It uses your resume and the job description to draft something professional, personal, and persuasive — no templates needed.

Best for

Best for job seekers who already have a resume but dread writing cover letters, or don’t have the time to customize one for every role. It’s especially helpful if you’re applying to multiple positions a week and need each letter to feel specific without starting from scratch.

Inputs & outputs

You bring a resume (pasted text or an uploaded file), the job description, and any notes about tone or emphasis. Letter Composer returns an editable cover letter you can refine in the chat and then export as DOCX, PDF, or plain text for your application portal or email.

Core functions (Leaves)

The specific jobs Letter Composer can run for you, again and again.

  • 📝 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Draft cover letters from resume + job description: Generate a first draft that connects your skills and experience directly to the requirements in the posting, without sounding like a boilerplate template.
  • 🎯 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Customize tone and focus: Shift the voice from enthusiastic to formal, people‑focused to technical, and highlight different parts of your story depending on the company and level.
  • 🔗 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Pull examples from your resume into the letter body: Automatically weave in specific projects, metrics, and achievements from your resume so the letter feels grounded and credible.
  • 🧹 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Clean up grammar and formatting: Tighten language, fix typos, and keep structure scannable for hiring managers and applicant tracking systems.
  • 📄 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Export polished letters in DOCX, TXT, or PDF: Package your final letter in the formats most online portals and recruiters expect, ready to upload or paste.

Why use this instead of vanilla ChatGPT?

What you get here that a blank ChatGPT window won’t give you.

Pre‑tuned brain

Letter Composer is pre‑wired to think in resume + job description pairs. It knows how to map requirements to your experience, suggest concrete examples, and stay within professional tone bands so you don’t have to keep repeating the same instructions in every conversation.

Suite‑aware & ecosystem‑ready

Because it lives on the Discovered Careers branch, Letter Composer shares language and structure with Resume Builder, Resume Customizer, Career Fitness, bLinkIn Tuner, and Career Seeker. Your core profile, achievements, and career narrative can carry across the suite instead of being rebuilt from scratch in separate chats.

Repeatable, export‑friendly workflows

Once you like a cover‑letter pattern, you can reuse the same structure for future roles: same intro, updated hook, refreshed examples. Letter Composer helps you keep track of versions, remember what worked, and export clean copies for each application so your process feels consistent instead of chaotic.

Getting started

Get from blank screen to a solid cover‑letter draft in just a few minutes.

  1. Step 1 – Bring your resume and one job post. Copy in your latest resume (or a version from Resume Builder/Customizer) and a real job description you want to apply to.
  2. Step 2 – Paste or upload both. Drop the resume and job post into the chat or upload them as files, label which is which, and tell Letter Composer the tone you’re going for.
  3. Step 3 – Tune the angle. Ask Letter Composer to emphasize what matters most for this role — leadership, technical depth, culture fit, or a career pivot — and iterate on the draft together.
  4. Step 4 – Export and reuse. Export the final letter as DOCX, PDF, or plain text, and reuse the same pattern for your next application so each new letter takes less time.
