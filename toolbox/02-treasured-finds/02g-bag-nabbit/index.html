Bag Nabbit

A cheeky little bag tracker for every purse, tote, backpack, and clutch you own.

Bag Nabbit helps you track, tag, and actually use the bags you already own — not just the ones you remember in the moment. It keeps a living log of what you have, where it lives, and when you last carried it so you can rotate favorites, avoid duplicates, and spot gaps in your collection.

Launch “Bag Nabbit” in ChatGPT Back to Glee‑fully Treasured Finds

Opens in ChatGPT in a new tab. Best experienced with your notes, lists, or files handy.

Core functions (Leaves)

These are the concrete ways Bag Nabbit keeps your bag collection organized and easy to use.

  • 👜 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf): Add bags with smart intake. Add bags manually or with photo uploads so Bag Nabbit can suggest details and keep entries consistent.
  • 🎯 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf): Tag by style, season, and mood. Classify each bag by style, color, brand, season, occasion, or mood so you can filter for “exactly what I need” in a few seconds.
  • 📝 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf): Capture purchase details and memories. Log price, purchase date, gift origin, or the story behind the bag so the history stays attached to the item, not lost in your inbox.
  • 📚 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf): Track storage, condition, and last‑worn dates. Keep a record of where each bag lives, how it’s holding up, and when you last used it so nothing disappears to the back of the closet.
  • 🔄 FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf): Manage rotation, wishlists, and loaned bags. Mark bags as in rotation, retired, wishlist, or loaned to a friend so you always know what’s active, what’s missing, and what needs to come home.

Why use this instead of vanilla ChatGPT?

Here’s what you get with a purpose‑built bag catalog instead of a blank ChatGPT window.

Pre‑tuned brain

Bag Nabbit is already wired to think in “bag data” — type, color, brand, storage spot, condition, and last‑worn date — so you don’t have to invent a tracking system from scratch or explain it over and over.

Suite‑aware & ecosystem‑ready

As part of Glee‑fully Treasured Finds, Bag Nabbit speaks the same language as your other catalog Tool‑ettes (books, decor, gifts, scents, and more), so your collections can play nicely together instead of living in isolated lists.

Repeatable, export‑friendly workflows

Every cataloging session can end in clean tables and checklists you can copy into spreadsheets, Notion, or docs — making your bag collection easy to review, share, or update instead of stuck in one‑off chat replies.

Getting started

Follow these steps to get a real, usable bag log up and running in just a few minutes.

  1. Step 1 – Grab 3–5 bags you actually use. Start small: everyday tote, work bag, weekend crossbody — whatever you reach for most.
  2. Step 2 – Tell Bag Nabbit what you’ve got. Paste a quick list or type one bag at a time with name, type, color, brand, and where you store it.
  3. Step 3 – Let it suggest tags and structure. Ask Bag Nabbit to recommend tags for season, occasion, and mood, then tweak anything that doesn’t feel quite like you.
  4. Step 4 – Ask for a collection view. Have it build a table of your bags so you can spot duplicates, gaps, and which pieces deserve more time in rotation.
  5. Step 5 – Save the output somewhere you’ll keep using it. Copy the table into your notes, spreadsheet, or task system so your bag collection lives somewhere more permanent than a single chat.
