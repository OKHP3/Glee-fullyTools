Decor Detective

Your seasonal décor memory, so the bins stop winning.

Decor Detective turns closets, attics, and storage tubs into a searchable, sharable catalog. Track what you own, where it lives, and when it comes out to play—from Halloween pumpkins to winter garlands. Built on the Glee‑fully Toolbox, it keeps your spaces festive without the yearly guesswork.

Launch “Decor Detective” in ChatGPT Back to Treasured Finds

Opens in ChatGPT in a new tab. Best experienced with your notes, lists, or files handy.

What this Tool‑ette does

A focused GPT that keeps track of every wreath, string light, and seasonal accent so you always know what you own and where it lives.

Core purpose

Decor Detective helps you build a simple, structured inventory of everything you hang, stack, or plug in to make a space feel festive. Instead of digging through mystery bins, you get a clear record of what you own, how it’s tagged, and which holidays or seasons it belongs to.

Best for

Ideal for people who rotate décor throughout the year—homeowners, renters, teachers, small retailers, or anyone with “just one more” bin in the garage. If you’ve ever bought duplicate string lights because you forgot what you already had, this is for you.

You bring, it handles

You bring: your rough lists, bin labels, photos turned into text, or “stuff I think is in the attic” notes.
It handles: clean catalog structures, smart tags by holiday, room, style, and use, storage locations, reminders, and maintenance notes you can reuse every season.

Core functions (Leaves)

The specific actions Decor Detective can take so your seasonal décor stays organized, visible, and easy to reuse.

  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Catalog decorations by holiday. Sort items like tree ornaments, lights, garlands, pumpkins, and seasonal tableware by holiday or season.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Tag by type, style, or use. Mark pieces as table settings, wall art, mantle décor, front‑porch items, or color themes so you can filter exactly what you need.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Track storage locations. Log which bin, shelf, closet, attic corner, or shed a set of items lives in so you can walk straight to it later.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Setup & takedown reminders. Build gentle reminders for when to decorate and when to pack things away, including recurring dates by holiday or season.
  • FUNCTION⚙️ (🍃Leaf) – Maintenance & condition logs. Note when items were cleaned, repaired, or retired so you can plan replacements and avoid last‑minute surprises.

Why use this instead of vanilla ChatGPT?

What you get here that you won’t get from a cold, one‑off prompt to vanilla ChatGPT.

Pre‑tuned brain

Decor Detective is wired around décor, not generic to‑do lists. It comes with a baked‑in schema for holidays, rooms, bins, tags, and condition fields, so you don’t have to re‑explain how your storage works every single chat.

Suite‑aware & ecosystem‑ready

As part of the Treasured Finds branch, Decor Detective understands how your décor inventory connects to gifts (Present Hoarder), collections (Personal Librarian), and sensory memories (Scentinal Journal). It plays nicely with the whole Glee‑fully tree, not just this one twig.

Designed for real life

It expects half‑finished lists, voice‑to‑text rambles, and “I think this bin is fall stuff?” guesses. The GPT is tuned to clean up messy inputs, ask clarifying questions, and return neat tables you can copy straight into your own systems.

How to start using Decor Detective

A simple first pass that turns one overloaded bin or holiday into a clean, reusable inventory.

  1. Step 1 – Start with one holiday or bin. Pick a single holiday, season, or storage bin—“Christmas tree stuff,” “Fall porch box,” or whatever feels manageable.
  2. Step 2 – Paste or describe what you own. Type or paste any existing list, or just describe what’s inside: “5 strands of warm‑white lights, 1 wreath with red ribbon, 2 glass reindeer…”. Decor Detective will turn it into structured rows.
  3. Step 3 – Choose a path. Tell the GPT whether you want to build a master inventory, plan this year’s setup/takedown checklist, or clean up tags and storage locations. It will adapt the questions and outputs for that mode.
  4. Step 4 – Save what matters. Copy the tables into your spreadsheet, Notion, or notes app, or ask for an export‑ready format (CSV‑style text, checklists by room, or printable packing labels) so you can reuse it every season.
